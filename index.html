About Me

Hi! My name is Amon, and I am an Associate Researcher in European Law at the Jean Monnet Centre of Excellence at the Federal University of Minas Gerais (starting in March 2024), working with Prof. Jamile Bergamaschine Mata Diz. I earned my LL.M. in European and International Law from Universität des Saarlandes, where I worked with Prof. Andreas R. Ziegler. I received my B.A. (Hons.) in Public Policy and Management (Magna Cum Laude) from the Federal University of Ceará, working with Prof. Gil Cardoso and Prof. Tarin Mont'Alverne.

My research interest is in understanding the intersection between public policies and international law. Specifically, I am currently working on the energy transition and the change of the energy matrix within the Brazilian and European contexts. In order to develop these studies, I employ a variety of methods. On one hand, I work with the Observatory of Sustainable Municipal Public Policies to produce annual reports on the situation of municipalities in the State of Minas Gerais regarding these specific themes.

I grew up in Fortaleza, Brazil, which is where I managed to enter higher education at a federal public university.

EU-MERCOSUR AGREEMENT & THE PRECAUTIONARY PRINCIPLE

EMPIRICAL LEGAL STUDIES ON NON-TARIFF BARRIERS

The European Union increasingly incorporates the Precautionary Principle into the Trade and Sustainable Development chapters of Free Trade Agreements. My research investigates whether these measures promote inclusive economic cooperation or act as disguised non-tariff barriers that disadvantage emerging economies, particularly in the context of the EU-Mercosur Association Agreement. Using a Python-based jurimetrics approach, I evaluate Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU) judgments between 2000 and 2023 to quantify the internal judicial rigor applied to this principle and contrast it with external trade enforcement. This work has been published in the Latin American Journal of European Studies.

ENERGY SECURITY AND THE EUROPEAN GREEN DEAL

GEOPOLITICAL TENSIONS AND JUST TRANSITION

The transition to a carbon-neutral economy by 2050 under the European Green Deal faces complex challenges. I analyze the paradox of the European Union's energy agreements, specifically focusing on the dynamics of natural gas trade relations with Azerbaijan following the Russia-Ukraine conflict. Despite efforts to promote renewable energy, the strategic dependence on fossil fuels creates a conflict between energy security, ethical principles, and human rights. My work highlights the need to integrate strict human rights policies into international agreements to ensure a truly sustainable and just transition. This work has been published in Monções: Revista de Relações Internacionais da UFGD.

Outreach

PUBLIC TALKS & PRESENTATIONS

I have had the privilege of presenting my research at several international conferences and academic events. Special highlights include discussions on international maritime law and European Union trade policies!

University of Lisbon (April 2026)

In April 2026, I presented at the University of Lisbon alongside my colleague Melina Coelho Garcia. Our talk was based on our article "Fair Trade or Hidden Barriers? Legal challenges from TFEU to FTAs for inclusive economic cooperation within the precautionary principle". We analyzed the European Union's commercial policies, focusing specifically on the EU-Mercosur agreement. Our discussion centered on the application of the precautionary principle, evaluating whether it serves as a mechanism for inclusive economic cooperation or acts as a hidden non-tariff barrier, given its explicit use in certain agreements and implicit presence in others.

22nd Brazilian Congress of International Law (August 2024)

In August 2024, I spoke at the 22nd Brazilian Congress of International Law. My presentation, titled "State Conflicts and Life at Sea: The Case of Switzerland v. Nigeria in International Law,"[cite: 3] explored the San Padre Pio case. I discussed the international dispute surrounding the seizure of a Swiss vessel in Nigeria's Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ). The case, which was taken to the International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea, raises critical questions about transit rights, the economic and environmental implications of vessels, and the overall legitimacy of EEZ regulations.
